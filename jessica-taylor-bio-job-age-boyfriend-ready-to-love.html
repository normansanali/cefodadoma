Who Are Jessica Taylor Parents?

Jessica Taylor has also shared stories of her mom on her Facebook. In February 2023, Jessica shared that growing up her mom would make Jessica and her sister walk to the mailbox every week to check the mail. Upon their return, the first thing she would always ask for was her Essence magazine.

She added, “No one could read it, or open it before her and she never threw them away. I can only imagine the joy she would have right now to see her very own in her favorite publication. Here’s to Joyce, here’s to hard work, here’s to Ezra, and here’s to BLACK HISTORY MONTH.”

This year, 2023, marked six years of losing her mother. In 2022, Jessica wrote, “One thing she would ask me in response to anything I had an idea or plan about (trust I had many) was “So what’s stopping you, besides you?” And my answer was always the same… “nothing”. So whatever it is your thinking about, DO IT. Do it scared, do it broke, do it by yourself I promise you won’t regret it.”

On Father’s Day 2018, Jessica wished her dad, “Happy Father’s Day to my one and only!😘.”

Jessica Taylor Job

Jessica Taylor is an entrepreneur and founder of Ezra Coffee. It all began when Jessica’s grandfather was persuaded by her interest in the potent beverage to give her a sneaky sip of his coffee. Despite her grandmother’s objections, Jessica developed a passion for coffee as a child that she has kept with her throughout her life.

Jessica decided to combine her love of equality, storytelling, and coffee into one business, Ezra Coffee, after developing a career working with organizations like Ohio State University and Perdue to promote diverse hiring.

The premium, responsibly sourced coffee company is committed to sharing the authentic narratives of significant Black personalities. Intentionally naming each flavor after Black leaders or significant cultural occurrences, Jessica also included a little narrative on each flavor’s packaging for customers to read. Among Ezra’s most popular blends include Lorde Baldwin, a potent Kenyan mix that pays homage to literary greats James Baldwin and Audre Lorde, and 64th & Tulsa, a medium dark roast that celebrates the significance of Black Wall Street.

“My goal with Ezra Coffee Co. is to always highlight the stories, legacies, and experiences of African-Americans who have contributed to our collective American history,” Jessica said. “With each cup of bold and flavorful coffee, Ezra [Coffee] informs, empowers, and inspires coffee connoisseurs and novices everywhere.”

In just two years, the brand has secured nationwide distribution in Target.com and H.E.B. stores nationwide.

“Ezra is a premium brand and each bag comes from the heart,” she told ESSENCE. “I really believe customers can taste that. I’m also integrating the stories that are pivotal to Black into the DNA of the brand, which is incredibly important. Our stories deserve to be told in a beautiful, thoughtful way.”

Some of Ezra’s best-selling blends include 64th & Tulsa, Lorde Baldwin, Le Grand Duc 1928, and Toasted Southern Pecan.
