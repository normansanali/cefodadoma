Who is Ann Akinjirin? Moon Knight’ Bobby

Ann Akinjirin (born on May 28, 1981) is a 40 years old British actress who lately saw a steep rise in her fame after she landed the role of a cop named Bobby in Marvel’s new blockbuster “Moon Knight.”

In this Disney+ series, an antisocial personality disorder-affected former US Marine is gifted the abilities of an Egyptian moon god. However, Marc Spector quickly discovers that his new abilities may be a mixed blessing.

In addition to Akinjirin, there is Oscar Isaac who is playing the role of Moon Knight alongside May Calamawy, Gaspard Ulliel, Antonia Salib, and many other skilled actors in this creation of Doug Moench.

As per one of the interviews with Variety, the lady said that many people expected her to be in Wakanda, but she is happy that she got to work with MCU and is not being treated differently because of her black skin and shaved head.

Akinjirin, other than being an actress, is an experienced Artistic Director, Writer, Director, and Artistic Director. She has been associated with the British acting industry since 2000 and has worked in numerous shows/films.

Beginning of Acting Career

Everything went smoothly until completing her studies, but the real struggle started when she began giving auditions for different production. Ann’s creative mind motivated her to explore new possibilities. Seeking to gain a foothold in the entertainment industry, Kinjirin started her own HARTS Theatre Company CIC in August 2013, of which she was also a director until January 2020.

2013 also became a year in which she was in the role of Cecile Bakama for season 7 of the crime series called “Law & Order: the UK,” which led to more opportunities in the coming years.

Ann did a total of 21 shows, including “Give Out Girls,” “Valentine’s Kiss,” “The Javone Prince Show,” “You, Me and the Apocalypse,” “Unforgotten,” “The Salisbury Poisonings,” and “I May Destroy You.” She also worked her fair share in short movies like “That Phone Call,” “All the Devil’s Men,” and “Down Dog.”

Its the determination, talent, and persistence that Ann Akinjirin has become able to achieve so much in her life. She is also doing her best to pay back to society. There is an organization called Deafinitely Theatre, with which Akinjirin is involved as a Movement Director and creates plays for deaf people.

Early Life and Family

The birth of Anne Akinjirin (age 40 years) took place to a British family of African origin on May 28, 1981, in London, UK. Her father & mother are originally from Nigeria and later came to the English nation searching for a better life.

Ann Akinjirin grew up watching movies like “Trainspotting,” “our Weddings and a Funeral,” and “Shakespeare in Love,” which motivated her to choose acting as a career path. Beginning her career, Akinjirin joined The BRIT School in 2000, where she did a diploma in Musical theatre, which got completed in 2002.

A year later, keeping the struggle on, she decided to pursue a Bachelor’s degree in media and performance from the University of Bedfordshire. Later, she did her post-graduation from Drama Studio London.

While at Bedfordshire, she cleared auditions for the role of Nurse Sarah Michael and performed in a total of 3 episodes sprinkled over different years.
