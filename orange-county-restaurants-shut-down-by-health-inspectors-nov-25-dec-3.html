Orange County restaurants shut down by health inspectors (Nov. 25-Dec. 3)

Publish date: 2024-09-18

Restaurants and other food vendors ordered to close and allowed to reopen by Orange County health inspectors from Nov. 25 to Dec. 3.

The Station Liquor, 16471 McFadden Ave., Tustin

  • Closed: Dec. 1
  • Reason: Cockroach infestation
  • Reopened: Dec. 1

Harbor Pizza, 13917 Harbor Blvd., Garden Grove

  • Closed: Dec. 1
  • Reason: Cockroach infestation

Food sales at Five Below, 586 N. Euclid St., Anaheim

  • Closed: Nov. 30
  • Reason: Rodent infestation
  • Reopened: Nov. 8

Brewberry Cafe, 1198 W. Katella Ave., Anaheim

  • Closed: Nov. 30
  • Reason: Rodent infestation

Carniceria “El Toro Neza”, 1534 S. Greenville St., Santa Ana

  • Closed: Nov. 30
  • Reason: Cockroach infestation
  • Reopened: Nov. 30

Char-O-Chicken, 333 Main St., Seal Beach

  • Closed: Nov. 29
  • Reason: Cockroach infestation
  • Reopened: Nov. 30

Updates since last week’s list:

Mr. Pollo Fresco at 12021 Garden Grove Blvd., Garden Grove, which was ordered closed Nov. 19 because of a rodent infestation, was allowed to reopen Nov. 30.

This list is published weekly with closures since the previous week’s list. Status updates are published in the following week’s list. Source: OC Health Care Agency database.
