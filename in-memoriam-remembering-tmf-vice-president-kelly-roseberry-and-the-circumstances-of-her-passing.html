In Memoriam: Remembering TMF Vice President Kelly Roseberry and the Circumstances of Her Passing. -

Publish date: 2024-09-30

News: The devastating passing of Kelly Roseberry, a cherished member of the Travis Mills Foundation (TMF), has created a profound emptiness in the hearts of many. Roseberry was not only an essential presence within the TMF but also a wellspring of inspiration for countless veterans and their families.

Beloved Member of the Travis Mills Foundation

Kelly Roseberry, born in Fort Lewis, Washington, stood as a beacon of hope for war veterans and injured military personnel. Serving as the Vice President of Programming at the TMF, she held a pivotal position within the organization. Roseberry brought a wealth of knowledge, with an educational foundation in nutrition, foods, and exercise from Virginia Tech, complemented by a doctorate degree in physical therapy from Shenandoah University.

Invaluable Contributions to The Family Program

Roseberry’s association with the TMF commenced in 2017, a journey she undertook alongside her husband. Throughout her tenure, she made substantial contributions to The Family Program by customizing activities to cater to the requirements and preferences of 9/11 veterans. Her engagement was far more than just administrative; it was profoundly personal. As a physical therapist and center coordinator of clinical education, she played a hands-on role in aiding veterans in their process of reintegration and in regaining real-life capabilities. The TMF duly recognized her invaluable contributions and conveyed their profound gratitude for her in their obituary.

A Multi-Talented Individual

In addition to her vital position at the TMF, Roseberry took on multiple roles. She generously volunteered her time as a personal trainer and amputee specialist at the Veterans Administration Hospital, highlighting her unwavering dedication to the cause. Her commitment extended to her role as an Adaptive Ski instructor and as the director of the Ski Camp for Amputee Kids. Furthermore, she served as an adjunct faculty member at several universities, imparting her wealth of knowledge in physical therapy and occupational therapy programs, further emphasizing her dedication to the field.

A Tragic Loss

The community was devastated on October 28 when reports confirmed the tragic passing of Kelly Roseberry and her son, Noah, in a car accident. This news prompted an outpouring of grief, with community members describing her as a “loving wife, grateful mother, daughter, fierce veteran advocate, patriotic, and loyal friend.”

A Lasting Legacy

The impact Roseberry had on the lives of many is evident in the heartfelt tributes that have flooded social media platforms. Countless community members took to Facebook to express their sorrow and share cherished memories of the 38-year-old. Her unwavering support for wounded and disabled servicemen, as well as her dedication to the organization, earned her wide admiration.

In memory of Kelly Roseberry, her family has requested that donations be made to the TMF.

An Unwavering Support and Dedication

Although her untimely passing is an immense loss, Kelly Roseberry leaves behind an immeasurable legacy that serves as a testament to her unwavering support and dedication to veterans’ causes. Her contributions will continue to inspire many for years to come.
