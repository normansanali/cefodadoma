Does size of high school matter for getting noticed by colleges?

Publish date: 2024-07-11

Choosing the right high school is crucial for students who aspire to play sports at the college level. Many student-athletes believe that transferring to a larger high school will increase their chances of getting noticed by college recruiters. However, the size of a high school is just one factor that college coaches consider when recruiting student-athletes. In this article, we will explore whether the size of a high school matters for getting noticed by colleges, and what other factors student-athletes should consider when choosing a high school.

Firstly, it is important to note that college coaches are looking for well-rounded student-athletes who can contribute to their team both on and off the field. While success in sports is certainly a factor that coaches consider, it is not the only measure of a student-athlete's potential. Coaches also look for students who are hardworking, dedicated, and have a strong academic record. Therefore, transferring to a larger high school solely for the purpose of getting noticed by college recruiters may not always be the best decision.

That being said, larger high schools do have some advantages when it comes to sports. They often have more resources and facilities, which can lead to better coaching and training opportunities. Additionally, larger high schools may have more competitive sports programs, which can help student-athletes improve their skills by playing against top-level competition. These factors can certainly be attractive to college recruiters, but they are not the only factors that coaches consider.

In fact, many successful college and professional athletes come from smaller high schools with less competitive sports programs. For example, NFL quarterback Drew Brees attended a high school with only 1,600 students, and NBA star Steph Curry attended a high school with only 350 students. These athletes were able to get noticed by college recruiters because they excelled in their sport and had strong academic records, despite attending smaller high schools.

Another factor to consider is the location of a high school. Colleges tend to recruit heavily from certain regions, so attending a high school in a state or region with a strong sports culture can be advantageous. For example, if a student-athlete wants to play football at the college level, attending a high school in Texas or Florida, where football is deeply ingrained in the culture, may be beneficial. However, this does not mean that student-athletes from other regions cannot be successful in their sport. It simply means that they may need to work harder to get noticed by college recruiters.

Ultimately, the size of a high school is just one of many factors that student-athletes should consider when choosing a high school. Other factors such as the quality of coaching, the strength of the sports program, and the academic reputation of the school should also be taken into account. Additionally, student-athletes should consider their long-term goals and aspirations beyond sports. While playing sports at the college level can be a rewarding experience, it is important to have a backup plan in case a sports career does not work out.

One way for student-athletes to increase their chances of getting noticed by college recruiters is to take an active role in the recruitment process. This means reaching out to college coaches, attending college camps and showcases, and creating a highlight reel or recruiting video to showcase their skills. It also means maintaining strong grades and test scores, as these are important factors that college coaches consider when recruiting student-athletes.

In addition to taking an active role in the recruitment process, student-athletes should also look for a high school that supports growth and development both on and off the field. This means finding a school with a strong academic program that will prepare them for college, as well as a sports program that will help them develop their skills and compete at a high level. It also means finding a school with a supportive community that will help them navigate the challenges of being a student-athlete.

In conclusion, while the size of a high school can certainly be a factor in getting noticed by college recruiters, it is not the only factor. Coaches are looking for well-rounded student-athletes who can contribute to their team both on and off the field. Therefore, student-athletes should consider a variety of factors when choosing a high school, including the quality of coaching, the strength of the sports program, the academic reputation of the school, and the location of the school. Additionally, student-athletes should take an active role in the recruitment process and find a high school that supports growth and development both on and off the field. By considering these factors, student-athletes can increase their chances of achieving their goals both in sports and in life.
