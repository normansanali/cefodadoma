How did Jim Acosta start his Professional Career?

Jim’s professional career began in radio. He started his career as a local radio host for WSVA. Following that, he went on to work for WMAL in Washington, D.C. He began his television career as an assistant at WTTG-TV after leaving radio.

In 1994, he made his television debut as a substitute anchor for WBIR Tv in Knoxville. Costa later worked for KTVT TV and WBBM. His first big break came when he worked as a correspondent for CBS News from 2001 to 2003.

From Hurricane Katrina to the Iraq war and John Kerry’s presidency, he covered major news events.

Acosta later left CBS in April 2007 to join CNN, where he covered the 2008 presidential elections, campaigns, and other major political events.
